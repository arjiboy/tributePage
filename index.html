Jack Ma

Businessman. Leader. Normal Guy.

Just A Normal Guy

Jack Ma is a Chinese businessman best known as the founder and executive chairman of Alibaba Group, a family of successful e-commerce-based businesses. Alibaba Group enabled small and medium enterprises from all over the world (initially with China) to do business with one another, allowing a vast ecosystem of buyers and sellers to thrive and grow their businesses. Along with Alibaba's success, Ma also grew in popularity and reputation as one of the most respected persons in the business world. His accomplishments were recognized worldwide. Year after year he received awards from renowned organizations, some of which were as follows:

  • Fortune Magazine selected him as one of the "25 Most Powerful Businessperson in Asia".
  • Businessweek also selected him as a "Businessperson of the Year" in 2007.
  • Barron's chose him as one of the 30 "World’s Best CEOs" in 2008.
  • Time magazine selected Ma as one of the world’s 100 most powerful people in 2009.
  • in 2014, he was ranked as the 30th most powerful person in the world in an annual ranking published by Forbes.
  • In 2015, Asian Award honoured him with the Entrepreneur of the Year award.
  • In 2017, Fortune ranked Ma second on its World's 50 Greatest Leaders list.

With all his achievements and accomplishments, Ma remains down-to-earth that he even called himself "just a normal guy".

From Rejections To Becoming a Businesman

Coming from humble beginnings, his early life was also marked by failures and rejections. In several of his interviews, Ma repeatedly told the same stories of his experiences before Alibaba and even during its early years, emphasizing that he is not special for him to have achieved his current stature.

  • At a young age, Ma would practice English daily by conversing with tourists. He would give them tours around the city for free to improve his English. He did this for 9 years.
  • In his youth, it took him 4 years to pass the college entrance exams. He graduated in 1988 with a B.A. in English.
  • Ma applied to 30 different jobs and was rejected by all. He even told a story where he and 23 others applied in KFC in his city and he was the only one rejected.
  • In addition, he applied for Harvard 10 times and got rejected.

Full of optimism for the future, Ma didn't let these challenges stop him. In 1994, he was introduced to the internet and saw an opportunity.An opportunity that would later lead to the formation of Alibaba after several attempts to be successful in the tech industry. With no programming knowledge (he "never typed a single line of code"), he led and started Alibaba with a group of 18 friends along with 500,000 yuan.

In September 2014, Alibaba became one of the most valuable tech companies in the world after raising $25 billion, the largest initial public offering in US financial history.
